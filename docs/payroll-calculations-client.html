1Pay Cycle & Working Days

Pay Period

The payroll cycle does not follow the standard calendar month. Instead, it runs from the 16th of the previous month to the 15th of the current month.

📅
Example: For the February 2026 payroll, the pay period is January 16 – February 15, 2026.

Working Days

Working days are all calendar days in the period excluding Saturdays and Sundays. This number is used as the base for all per-day rate calculations.

// Working Days Formula Working Days = All days in period − Saturdays − Sundays // Typical result: ~22 days per month
⚠️
Public holidays are not automatically excluded. An employee on a public holiday should be marked with the appropriate attendance status (e.g., "present" or "holiday").

2Salary Structure & Breakdown

Each employee has a Standard Salary (their total monthly pay). This is automatically split into components using fixed percentages:

Component Percentage Example (PKR 50,000)
Basic Salary 50% 25,000
House Rent Allowance 20% 10,000
Utilities Allowance 15% 7,500
Bike Maintenance Allowance 10% 5,000
Mobile Allowance 5% 2,500
Total 100% 50,000

Additional Fixed Allowances

These are set individually per employee and are not part of the percentage breakdown. They are also generally not affected by attendance deductions:

Allowance Description
Fuel Allowance Fixed monthly amount for fuel costs
Special Allowance Fixed monthly special compensation
Conveyance Allowance Daily commute allowance — partially affected by approved leaves (see Section 4)

3Attendance Categories

Each working day in the pay period is assigned one of the following statuses. The status determines how (or whether) a deduction is applied.

Status Description Deduction Applied?
Present Employee attended. May have full or short hours. Only if hours are short (undertime)
Absent Employee did not show up (unexcused). ✅ Yes — full day deduction
Half Day Employee worked approximately half the shift. ✅ Yes — 50% day deduction
Leave Approved leave (treated as unpaid). ✅ Yes — conveyance only
Holiday Official public holiday. ❌ No deduction
ℹ️
Each attendance record also tracks actual duty hours worked and overtime hours logged, which feed into undertime deductions and overtime pay respectively.

4Deduction Rules

Attendance-based deductions are calculated by first establishing the daily and hourly rates for each salary component:

// Base Rates (calculated once per payslip) Per Day Basic = Basic Salary ÷ Total Working Days Per Hour Basic = Per Day Basic ÷ Standard Duty Hours (default: 8) Per Day [Component] = Component Amount ÷ Total Working Days // Example: Basic = 25,000 | Working Days = 22 | Duty Hours = 8 Per Day Basic = 25,000 ÷ 22 = 1,136.36 Per Hour Basic = 1,136.36 ÷ 8 = 142.05
Scenario 1

🚫 Full Day Absent

Employee did not attend. One full day's worth of Basic + 4 standard allowances is deducted.

Deduction = Per Day Basic + Per Day House Rent + Per Day Utilities + Per Day Bike Maint. + Per Day Mobile
Affects: 5 components
Scenario 2

🌗 Half Day

Employee worked approximately half the shift. Exactly 50% of the full absent deduction is applied.

Deduction = Full Day Deduction × 0.5
Affects: 5 components at 50%
Scenario 3

⏱️ Undertime (Short Hours)

Employee was present but worked fewer hours than required. Only Basic Salary is affected — allowances are untouched.

Short Hours = Standard Hours − Actual Hours Deduction = Per Hour Basic × Short Hours
Affects: Basic Salary only
Scenario 4

📋 Approved Leave

Employee is on approved leave. Since they didn't commute, only the daily portion of Conveyance Allowance is deducted.

Deduction = Per Day Conveyance
Affects: Conveyance only
💡
How it appears on the payslip: Attendance deductions are applied directly to each salary component. So the "Basic Salary" shown on the payslip is already the adjusted (post-deduction) amount — not the original.

5Overtime Calculation

When an employee works beyond their standard shift hours, those extra hours are compensated at 1.5× the hourly basic rate (commonly known as "time and a half").

// Overtime Formula Overtime Rate = Per Hour Basic × 1.5 Overtime Pay = Overtime Rate × Total OT Hours (summed across the month) // Example: Per Hour Basic = 142.05 | Total OT = 10 hours Overtime Rate = 142.05 × 1.5 = 213.08 Overtime Pay = 213.08 × 10 = PKR 2,131
ℹ️
Overtime hours are totalled across all days in the pay period. Overtime pay is added as an earning to the Gross Salary — it is never a deduction.

6Manual / Flat Deductions

In addition to attendance-based deductions, the payroll admin can apply flat deductions that are subtracted directly from the Gross Salary:

Type Description
Income Tax A flat amount withheld for tax purposes
Salary Advance Recovery of a loan or advance previously given to the employee
Manual Deductions Any custom items — e.g., damaged equipment, uniform cost, fines
Total Flat Deductions = Tax + Advance + Sum of all Manual Deductions

7Final Pay Formula

The following steps show exactly how the final net salary is calculated, in order:

1

Start with Salary Components

Basic Salary, House Rent, Utilities, Bike Maintenance, Mobile, Conveyance, Fuel, Special Allowance.

2

Apply Attendance Deductions

Loop through every attendance record. For each absent/half-day/short-hour/leave day, reduce the relevant salary components accordingly. Result: Adjusted Earnings.

3

Add Variable Earnings

Add Overtime Pay, Night Shift Allowance, Incentive Amount, and Bonus Amount to the adjusted earnings.

4

Calculate Gross Salary

Sum of all adjusted earnings + variable earnings = Gross Salary.

Gross = Adjusted Components + Overtime + Bonus + Incentive

5

Subtract Flat Deductions

Deduct Tax, Advance, and any Manual Deductions from the Gross Salary.

Total Deductions = Tax + Advance + Manual

6

Net Salary (Final Take-Home)

Net = max(0, Gross − Total Deductions)

Net salary can never be negative — it floors at zero.

9Automatic Validation Checks

The system automatically runs the following checks on every generated payslip before it is finalized:

Error
Negative Net Salary — Deductions exceed total earnings. Payslip cannot be generated.
Error
Attendance Overflow — Total attendance days (present + absent + leave + half-day) exceed the working days in the period.
Warning
Zero Gross Salary — Employee may be on full unpaid leave for the entire period.
Warning
Missing CNIC — Employee's national ID number is not on file.
Warning
Missing Bank Details — Employee's bank account number is not on file.
Warning
Excessive Deductions — Total deductions exceed 50% of the Gross Salary.
